The Problem
CareForward Group support workers spend 40 to 72 hours per week on manual administrative logging across a three-shift team. Staff spend 5 to 15 minutes every hour on device logging during a shift, competing directly with care delivery time. The most consistent feedback from operational staff: "more time logging than with people." Medication records carry the highest error risk and trigger costly escalation chains when errors occur. Early health deterioration is detected through informal verbal observation, unstructured and lost between shift changes.
Four AI Opportunities
1
Voice-to-Text Care Logging
Staff speak observations naturally. AI transcribes and populates the correct log category automatically. Human review before saving. No manual typing. No device navigation during care.
35-75 min/shift → 7-14 min/shift  |  £18,720-£46,800/yr saved
SHAP stability 0.9934 — every auto-populated field traceable to source speech input. CQC-auditable.
2
Medication Error Prevention
AI pre-administration cross-check against GP, pharmacist and SALT directives. Real-time MAR completeness flag. Refusal pattern tracking. Dual-witness human protocol unchanged.
60-80% error reduction  |  ROI greater than 10:1 vs implementation cost
LogAE anomaly detection — same mechanism as dissertation DoS detection, 0.95 F1 maintained under 30% feature corruption.
3
Early Health Deterioration Detection
AI learns each service user's personal baseline across mood, health, activity, fluid and sleep logs. Deviations trigger early advisory alerts to senior staff — before deterioration becomes acute.
40-60% earlier intervention  |  £5,000-£7,500/month in avoided admissions
Direct dissertation application: LogAE 0.95 accuracy, 0.99 AUC. SHAP identifies which features drove each alert.
4
AI-Assisted Handover Summaries
AI compiles shift logs into a structured handover template at shift end. SHAP surfaces the most significant entries automatically. Staff review and sign off. Consistent structure every shift.
15-30 min → 3-5 min  |  £6,240-£15,600/yr saved  |  Best pilot candidate
NLP summarisation with SHAP-prioritised content. Lowest risk. Builds staff confidence for Phase 2.

Why Federated Architecture
Federated — Recommended
Health data processed locally at each care home. Never transmitted.
GDPR special category obligations met by design.
0.95 accuracy matching centralised performance. Zero privacy trade-off.
Drift detection monitors each home independently.
Centralised — Not Recommended
Centralising individual health records increases GDPR breach exposure.
Data sharing agreements required across all homes.
Marginal accuracy advantage does not justify privacy risk.
Single point of failure for sensitive care data.
